## Why Heber City Properties See Severe Storm Damage

The geography here does most of the explaining. Heber City is ringed by the Uinta National Forest to the east and the Wasatch Range to the west, which means convective storms build fast and have nowhere to dissipate before they hit residential neighborhoods. The valley also experiences a pronounced freeze-thaw cycle from late September through May — a hailstorm in October can bruise shingles, and the first hard freeze a week later turns those micro-fractures into full splits that leak all winter.

The housing stock adds another layer of complexity. A significant portion of homes in the 84032 ZIP code were built in the 1970s and 1980s during Heber's first growth wave, and they commonly feature T-111 wood siding, shake roofs, and older window flashing details that weren't designed to handle the moisture loads a Wasatch Back thunderstorm delivers. Newer construction near Midway Road and the Soldier Hollow corridor tends to have better envelope performance, but it also sits on flatter terrain that collects runoff when storm drains back up. Neither vintage is immune.

Willow trees and mature cottonwoods — common throughout older residential blocks near the Provo River corridor — become liabilities in high-wind events. A single large limb through a roof deck can let in enough water in one night to saturate attic insulation, damage ceiling joists, and begin the 24–48 hour window in which mold colonization becomes a real concern.

## Our Storm Damage Restoration Process in Heber City

The first priority on any storm job is stopping ongoing water intrusion. That means emergency tarping and board-up before anything else — roof penetrations, broken windows, compromised siding. We carry full tarping kits on every truck so we're not waiting on a second vehicle.

Once the structure is stabilized, the process moves through three phases:

**Damage documentation and moisture mapping.** We photograph every affected surface and use thermal imaging cameras and calibrated moisture meters to trace water migration behind walls, under flooring, and into subfloor assemblies. Insurance adjusters for Utah homeowner policies increasingly require this level of documentation, and we build the file as we go.

**Debris removal and structural drying.** Fallen tree material, damaged roofing, and saturated insulation come out first. Then industrial air movers and desiccant dehumidifiers run continuously — typically 3 to 5 days depending on material depth and ambient humidity — until moisture readings return to pre-loss baselines. Heber's high-altitude, low-humidity air actually helps drying times compared to coastal climates, but the cold nights slow evaporation from dense materials like OSB sheathing.

**Repair and reconstruction.** Our IICRC-certified technicians carry the work through finish repairs: framing, sheathing, roofing underlayment, insulation, drywall, and paint matching. One point of contact, one invoice, one warranty.
