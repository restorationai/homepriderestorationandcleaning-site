## Why Park City Properties See Tile & Grout Issues

The water coming out of taps in the 84060 ZIP code is notably hard, consistently measuring high in calcium and magnesium carbonates. That mineral load leaves a chalky haze on glazed tile and slowly fills the microscopic pores of unsanded grout with scale that ordinary cleaners can't dissolve. Pair that with the grit that gets tracked in from ski boots, hiking trails, and the unpaved access roads around the Snyderville Basin, and grout lines become a fine-grained trap for abrasive particles that scratch tile glaze and grind the grout surface down over seasons.

Older condominiums and townhomes near the base of Park City Mountain Resort were often finished with natural stone tile — travertine, slate, and tumbled marble — that is far more porous than modern porcelain. Those materials absorb iron from hard water and tannins from tracked-in debris in ways that standard steam cleaning can make worse, not better, if the technician doesn't account for the stone type before choosing chemistry.

## Our Tile & Grout Cleaning Process in Park City

The work starts with a dry inspection under raking light to map stain types, grout condition, and whether any existing sealer is still intact. That step matters here because a failing silicone sealer traps moisture underneath it — a common finding in shower enclosures in high-use vacation rentals that see heavy turnover between ski season guests.

Once the surface is mapped, we apply a pH-balanced alkaline pre-treatment to emulsify body oils and soap scum in wet areas, and a separate mineral-acid solution — applied carefully and rinsed completely — to dissolve the calcium carbonate scale that's specific to Park City's water supply. We then run a truck-mounted rotary extraction tool over the floor or wall tile. The spinning heads deliver pressurized hot water directly into the grout channel and vacuum the suspended soil back out in the same pass, so the floor isn't left sitting in dirty water.

After cleaning and a drying interval, we apply a penetrating silane-siloxane sealer to grout lines. In a mountain climate with genuine humidity swings between a dry winter interior and a wet spring exterior, a penetrating sealer outperforms a topical coating because it bonds inside the pore structure rather than forming a film that can peel when moisture migrates through the substrate.

## Equipment & Methods We Use for Tile & Grout

The truck-mounted extraction unit runs water temperature above 200°F at the wand, which is necessary to break the bond between grout and the biofilm that forms in shower tile in vacation properties that sit unoccupied between rentals. Portable units used by many residential cleaners don't reach that temperature consistently at altitude — water boils at roughly 198°F at Park City's elevation, which affects the performance of steam-based equipment in ways that matter for sanitizing grout in wet areas.

For natural stone floors, we switch to a soft-bristle rotary head and stone-safe neutral cleaners rather than the standard tile brush and alkaline solution. Travertine and marble floors are common in higher-end homes and lodges throughout the area, and the wrong chemistry etches the surface permanently.
