## Why Herriman Homes Hold Odors Longer Than You'd Expect

Most of Herriman's residential construction has gone up since the early 2000s, which sounds like an advantage — newer materials, tighter building codes. In practice, it creates a specific deodorization challenge. Modern spray-foam and blown-in insulation, common in the subdivisions along Herriman's southern bench, traps air rather than cycling it. When a smoke or sewage event occurs, odor compounds saturate that stagnant air column and then absorb into every porous surface before the homeowner even realizes the extent of the problem.

Utah's climate adds another layer. The Salt Lake Valley's temperature inversions — especially pronounced in the colder months when cold air settles in the basin and traps particulates — mean that outdoor ventilation, the DIY fix most people try first, simply doesn't work the way it would in a coastal city with steady breezes. Opening windows in January or February in Herriman often pulls inversion-layer air inside rather than flushing odors out. Professional-grade equipment is the only reliable path forward.

## Our Odor Removal and Deodorization Process in Herriman

Every job starts with a source assessment — we don't treat symptoms before we've confirmed the cause. A technician walks the structure, identifies the odor type (protein-based from fire or cooking, microbial from mold or sewage, chemical from solvents or smoke residue), and maps the affected zones. That assessment drives the method selection.

For smoke odor removal after a fire or heavy cooking event, we typically deploy **thermal fogging**, which uses a petroleum-based or water-based deodorizer heated into a fog that penetrates the same microscopic pathways the smoke traveled. It's particularly effective in Herriman's newer homes because the same tight envelope that trapped the odor also holds the fog in contact with contaminated surfaces long enough to neutralize the odor molecules chemically.

For biological odors — pet urine, sewage backup, decomposition — we use **hydroxyl deodorization** or **ozone treatment** depending on occupancy. Ozone generators produce high concentrations of O₃ that oxidize odor compounds at the molecular level, but the space must be unoccupied during treatment. Hydroxyl machines are safe around people and pets, making them the right call when a family can't vacate. We carry both and make the call on-site based on your situation.

After treatment, we run air scrubbers with HEPA and activated-carbon filtration to capture any remaining particulates, then conduct a clearance check before we leave.
