## Why South Jordan Homes Are Vulnerable to Roof Leak Damage

The Jordan Valley's climate creates a specific problem for residential roofing: temperatures in the 84095 ZIP code routinely swing 30–40°F between day and night in March and April. That thermal cycling expands and contracts flashing, ridge caps, and sealant around skylights and HVAC penetrations. Over two or three seasons, those micro-gaps become pathways for water. South Jordan's newer master-planned communities — many built on the benchlands west of Mountain View Corridor — also feature low-slope rooflines and complex valley intersections that pool water rather than shedding it quickly.
