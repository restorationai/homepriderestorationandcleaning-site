## Local Note: What Riverton's Hard Water Does to Carpet After Cleaning

One thing that surprises Riverton homeowners who've had carpet cleaned before: rapid resoiling in the weeks after a service visit. The culprit is usually mineral residue, not poor cleaning. Riverton's municipal water supply, like most of the Salt Lake Valley, runs high in dissolved calcium and magnesium — hardness levels that can exceed 300 mg/L. When cleaning solution is rinsed with hard water and the carpet dries, those minerals precipitate out and leave a slightly tacky residue on fiber surfaces that attracts new soil faster than clean fiber would. We counter this with a low-pH fiber rinse as the final extraction pass, which neutralizes alkaline residue and leaves fibers genuinely clean rather than just visually clean. It's a small step that makes a measurable difference in how long results hold between cleanings.
