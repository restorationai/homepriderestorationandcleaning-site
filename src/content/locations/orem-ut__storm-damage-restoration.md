## Why Orem Properties See Distinctive Storm Damage Patterns

Orem's housing stock runs the full century — from mid-century brick ramblers near the center of town to newer vinyl-sided construction in developments along the east bench. That mix matters after a storm. Older brick homes absorb and hold moisture differently than modern framed walls; water that enters through a compromised roof or a cracked mortar joint can wick laterally inside a brick wythe for days without showing on the interior surface. Meanwhile, the valley's clay-heavy soil — common across the Utah Lake basin — drains slowly, which means standing water around foundations lingers long after the storm passes and keeps hydrostatic pressure against basement walls elevated.

Hail is a particular problem in the 84057 and 84058 ZIP code areas, where storms tracking northeast off the lake can drop golf-ball-size hail with little warning. That hail doesn't just crack shingles; it fractures gutters, punches through soffit venting, and drives water into attic insulation. Left unaddressed for even 24 to 48 hours, wet insulation becomes a mold-colonization environment — a secondary problem that costs more to fix than the original storm damage.

## Our Storm Damage Restoration Process in Orem

Every job starts with a documented scope — photos, moisture readings, and a written assessment — before any work begins. That documentation matters for insurance claims and, in some cases, for city building permits if structural repairs are needed.

**Emergency stabilization** comes first: tarping exposed rooflines, boarding compromised windows, and extracting any standing water from interior spaces. We use truck-mounted extraction units capable of pulling several hundred gallons per hour, which matters when a storm has pushed water across finished basement flooring or under hardwood.

**Structural drying** follows, using commercial-grade desiccant dehumidifiers and high-velocity air movers positioned to dry wall cavities, not just surface materials. We monitor moisture content daily with pin and pinless meters and don't close a job until readings return to pre-loss baselines — typically 7 to 14 days depending on material depth and ambient humidity.

**Debris and tree damage cleanup** runs parallel. A downed tree on a roof is both a water-intrusion problem and a structural load problem. We coordinate with licensed arborists for limb removal when needed, then assess the decking and framing beneath before any patching begins.

**Reconstruction** — from shingle replacement to full interior finish work — is handled in-house under Utah contractor license #RC-25-0737, so homeowners don't have to manage a separate general contractor.

## Orem Insurance Coordination

Most homeowner policies in Utah cover sudden and accidental storm damage, but the claim process has friction points that slow repairs. Adjusters often want a scope of damage before authorizing work; we provide a written assessment with photos that meets that standard. We work directly with adjusters from the major carriers active in Utah County — State Farm, Allstate, Farmers, and regional carriers like Bear River Mutual — and can supplement a claim if hidden damage (wet insulation, compromised sheathing) is found after initial inspection. We don't inflate scopes, and we don't pressure homeowners to file claims that don't warrant one.

## Local Note

Orem's older neighborhoods near Center Street have a concentration of homes built with original clay-tile roofing — a material that handles UV and dry heat well but becomes brittle under freeze-thaw cycling and shatters under hail impact rather than bending like asphalt shingles. When a tile roof takes a hit, the breakage pattern is less obvious from the ground than missing asphalt tabs, which means homeowners sometimes don't know they have an entry point until water shows up on the ceiling two or three storms later. If your home has clay or concrete tile and it's taken any hail in the last season, a post-storm inspection is worth scheduling even if you don't see visible damage from the yard.
