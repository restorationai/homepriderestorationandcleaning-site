## Why South Jordan Properties See Sewage Backup Issues

South Jordan sits atop expansive clay soils that shift seasonally — swelling with spring snowmelt and contracting through the dry summer months. That ground movement stresses buried lateral sewer lines year after year, eventually cracking joints or collapsing sections entirely. Many of the city's older neighborhoods were platted before Jordan Valley Water Conservancy District's current infrastructure standards, meaning some homes still carry original clay or Orangeburg pipe from the 1970s and early 1980s beneath their slabs. When those pipes fail, sewage doesn't just back up through floor drains — it can migrate laterally through the gravel bed under a concrete slab before surfacing, making the visible damage a fraction of the actual contamination zone.

Septic systems are another factor in South Jordan's more rural-edge parcels near the Bangerter Highway corridor. A saturated drain field after a heavy spring rain can force raw effluent back toward the home's foundation, creating an overflow event that looks and smells identical to a municipal sewer backup but requires a different remediation approach and coordination with Salt Lake County Environmental Health rather than the city's public works department.

## Our Sewage Cleanup and Sanitization Process in South Jordan

Every sewage call begins with containment — isolating the affected area to prevent cross-contamination to clean spaces before any extraction starts. Our IICRC-certified technicians pump standing sewage using truck-mounted extraction units, then remove all porous materials that cannot be decontaminated: saturated carpet, pad, drywall below the flood line, and compromised insulation. Nothing porous that has contacted Category 3 water gets dried in place.

Once the space is cleared, we apply EPA-registered hospital-grade disinfectants to all affected hard surfaces — concrete, framing lumber, subfloor sheathing — using a dwell-time protocol that ensures full pathogen kill rather than a surface wipe. Industrial air movers and desiccant dehumidifiers then run continuously, typically for three to five days, with moisture readings logged daily. In South Jordan's semi-arid climate, ambient outdoor humidity is generally low, which helps drying cycles move faster than in coastal markets — but that same dry air can mask residual odor issues until the space warms up, so we use thermal imaging to confirm no moisture pockets remain before closing out.
