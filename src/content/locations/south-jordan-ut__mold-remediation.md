South Jordan sits in a climate that looks dry on paper but hides a persistent moisture problem beneath the surface. The valley floor along the Bangerter Highway corridor collects irrigation runoff from the dense residential developments that have gone up since the early 2000s, and when a supply line fails or a roof flashing leaks, that moisture has nowhere to drain fast. Mold can begin colonizing porous materials — drywall paper, OSB sheathing, carpet backing — within 24 to 48 hours of a water event. If you're smelling something musty or seeing dark staining behind baseboards, the window to act without a major rebuild is already narrowing.

## Why South Jordan Properties See Mold Issues

Most of South Jordan's residential stock was built between 1998 and 2015, during a period when builders in Utah County and southern Salt Lake County were racing to keep up with population growth. Homes in that era commonly used paper-faced drywall throughout, including in bathrooms and laundry rooms where vapor management was an afterthought. That paper facing is exactly what mold eats first.

The area also sits on clay-heavy soils that drain poorly after heavy snowmelt or the late-summer monsoon rains that push up from the south each July and August. Crawl spaces and slab-on-grade foundations in subdivisions near the South Jordan Parkway corridor regularly trap ground moisture, especially when landscaping grades toward the foundation rather than away from it. Combine that with the tight construction envelopes that became standard after energy codes tightened in the mid-2000s — less air exchange means humidity that enters a home stays there longer.

## Our Mold Remediation Process in South Jordan

Every job starts with a moisture mapping inspection before a single piece of drywall comes out. We use thermal imaging and pin-type moisture meters to trace the water source, because treating visible mold without eliminating the moisture pathway guarantees a callback. Once the source is confirmed and stopped, we establish negative-air containment using 6-mil poly barriers and HEPA-filtered air scrubbers — this keeps spores from migrating to unaffected rooms during removal.

Demolition is limited to what's actually contaminated. We remove and bag affected materials following EPA guidelines, then apply an EPA-registered antimicrobial to framing and concrete surfaces. Clearance air testing — conducted by an independent industrial hygienist, not by us — confirms spore counts are within normal outdoor background levels before we close walls. That independence matters: you get an unbiased result, not a contractor grading their own work.

Home Pride Restoration and Cleaning has been IICRC Certified and operating since 1997, and our license (#RC-25-0737) covers the remediation and reconstruction work so you're not coordinating two separate contractors.
