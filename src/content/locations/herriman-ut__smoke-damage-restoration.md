## Why Herriman Homes Are Especially Vulnerable to Smoke Damage

High-desert conditions create a specific smoke damage profile. With relative humidity regularly dropping into the single digits during summer and early fall, smoke particulates lose their moisture carrier and settle as a fine, electrostatically charged film across every horizontal and vertical surface in a home — not just the room of origin. Herriman's rapid residential growth over the past two decades also means the housing stock skews toward large, open-concept floor plans with high ceilings and interconnected HVAC systems. ## Our Smoke Damage Restoration Process in Herriman

When a call comes in, the first priority is containment — stopping cross-contamination before it spreads to unaffected rooms. Our IICRC-certified technicians begin with a full scope assessment: identifying smoke type (wet smoke from smoldering fires, dry smoke from fast-burning fires, protein smoke from kitchen fires), mapping migration paths through the HVAC system, and testing surface pH to guide chemical selection for neutralization.

From there, the process moves through dry soot removal using HEPA-filtered vacuums and dry sponges, followed by wet cleaning with alkaline or acidic agents matched to the surface and smoke type. Porous materials — insulation, drywall, carpet padding — are evaluated for salvageability. Structural cavities are checked for hidden soot migration, which is common in Herriman's newer two-story homes where smoke travels up interior wall channels to attic spaces. Thermal fogging and hydroxyl generation are used for odor neutralization, and HVAC systems are cleaned and re-filtered before the system is returned to service.
