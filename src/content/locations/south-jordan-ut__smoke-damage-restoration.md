## Our Smoke Damage Restoration Process in South Jordan

When we arrive on-site — typically within 60 to 90 minutes of your call from our Saratoga Springs headquarters — the first priority is containment and assessment, not cleaning. Rushing into surface scrubbing before understanding smoke migration routes is one of the most common mistakes in post-fire cleanup, and it permanently sets odor compounds into porous materials.

Our process moves through these stages:

**1. Scope and air quality reading.** We use particle counters and thermal imaging to map where smoke traveled, including inside wall cavities and HVAC plenums. In South Jordan's newer construction, spray-foam insulation in rim joists can trap smoke in ways that aren't visible from the surface.

**2. Dry soot removal.** Chemical sponges and HEPA-filtered vacuums remove loose soot before any wet cleaning begins. Applying liquid cleaners to dry soot smears it deeper into drywall and grout.

**3. Chemical neutralization.** We use alkaline or acidic cleaning agents matched to the type of smoke — protein smoke from a kitchen fire requires a different chemistry than synthetic-material smoke from burning plastics or insulation.

**4. Thermal fogging and hydroxyl treatment.** For odor that has penetrated structural materials, we deploy hydroxyl generators or thermal foggers that replicate smoke's own behavior to reach the same surfaces smoke reached.

**5. HVAC decontamination.** South Jordan homes with whole-home air filtration systems need duct cleaning and filter replacement as a mandatory final step, not an optional add-on.
