## Our Frozen Pipe Restoration Process in Heber City

Restoration after a frozen pipe burst isn't just about mopping up standing water. The process has to account for what the water touched, how long it sat, and what materials it saturated — all of which vary depending on your home's construction and how quickly you called.

When a Home Pride crew arrives, the first priority is stopping any active water flow and documenting the full extent of damage with moisture meters and thermal imaging cameras. Thermal imaging is especially useful in Heber City homes with plaster or older drywall, where water wicks laterally through wall cavities and doesn't always show on the surface. Once the scope is mapped, we extract standing water, remove saturated insulation from crawl spaces or wall cavities, and set industrial drying equipment — high-capacity air movers and refrigerant dehumidifiers calibrated for high-altitude, low-humidity conditions.

At altitude, evaporation rates behave differently than at sea level. Our IICRC-certified technicians monitor moisture readings daily and adjust equipment placement until readings return to pre-loss baselines — not just until the floor feels dry to the touch.
