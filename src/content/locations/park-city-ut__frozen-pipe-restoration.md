## Our Frozen Pipe Restoration Process in Park City

When a thawed pipe releases water inside a Park City property, the clock matters more than it does at lower elevations. At 7,000 feet, ambient humidity is low — which sounds helpful, but it also means porous materials like concrete subfloors and log-style framing (common in resort-area construction) dry unevenly, with surface readings dropping while moisture stays trapped deep in the material. Our IICRC-certified technicians use thermal imaging cameras and penetrating moisture meters calibrated for dense wood and masonry to find water that a surface reading would miss.

The process moves in clear stages: contain the active water source, extract standing water with truck-mounted units, map the full moisture boundary, then set a drying system sized for the structure — not a one-size number. In Park City's dry mountain air, desiccant dehumidifiers often outperform refrigerant units because they work efficiently even when interior temps are still cold from a recently heated-up property. We document moisture readings at every stage for your insurance file, then re-inspect before packing out equipment.
