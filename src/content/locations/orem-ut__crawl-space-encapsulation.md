## Why Orem Crawl Spaces Are Especially Vulnerable

The housing stock along State Street's older residential corridors — many of those homes were built in the 1950s through 1970s — was constructed before vapor barriers were standard practice. Builders at the time laid foundations over native soil that, in Orem's climate zone, cycles between frozen and saturated multiple times each winter. That freeze-thaw action cracks block foundations and opens gaps where ground moisture migrates upward. Add in the pressurized irrigation systems that run through summer and you have a crawl space that never fully dries out on its own.

Orem also sits in a semi-arid climate that tricks homeowners: because the outdoor air feels dry, people assume moisture isn't a concern. But relative humidity inside an unencapsulated crawl space can run 70–80% even when it's 20% outside, because the soil itself is the moisture source. At 60% relative humidity, mold can begin colonizing wood framing within 48–72 hours of a leak or heavy rain event. Encapsulation breaks that cycle by isolating the soil from your living space entirely.

## Our Crawl Space Encapsulation Process in Orem

Every job starts with a full inspection — we measure the crawl space dimensions, check for standing water or existing mold, test ambient humidity, and photograph the existing vapor barrier (if any). In many Orem homes we find the original 6-mil poly sheeting, torn and unlapped, doing almost nothing.

From there the process follows a defined sequence:

1. **Debris removal and surface prep** — old vapor barrier, insulation batts that have fallen, and any organic debris come out first.
2. **Moisture and mold treatment** — if we find active mold on joists or sill plates, we address it before sealing anything in. Encapsulating over active mold only traps the problem.
3. **20-mil reinforced liner installation** — we use a heavy-duty, puncture-resistant liner rated for permanent installation. Seams are overlapped a minimum of 12 inches and taped with a compatible adhesive tape, not just folded.
4. **Wall and pier coverage** — the liner runs up foundation walls and wraps piers so ground moisture can't wick in from the sides.
5. **Drainage and ventilation assessment** — depending on your foundation type, we may recommend a dehumidifier sized for your crawl space square footage, or seal existing vents as part of a conditioned crawl space approach. Utah code allows both vented and unvented crawl space designs; we walk you through which fits your home.
6. **Final humidity reading and documentation** — you get before-and-after readings so you can see the difference.

## Local Note: Irrigation Season Changes Everything

One thing that surprises Orem homeowners is how much the pressurized irrigation season — roughly April through October — affects crawl space conditions. Yards in established neighborhoods along 400 North and 800 North are watered heavily through the summer, and that water doesn't all evaporate or run off. A significant portion percolates down to the foundation level, especially in lots with clay-heavy soil common to the valley floor. We've encapsulated crawl spaces in late September that were bone dry by inspection standards, only to find the liner holding back visible condensation by the following May when irrigation kicked back on. If you're scheduling encapsulation work, completing it before irrigation season starts — or immediately after it ends — gives you the clearest picture of what you're dealing with and the best long-term result.
