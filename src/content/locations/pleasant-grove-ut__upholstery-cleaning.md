## Why Pleasant Grove Upholstery Wears the Way It Does

The same high-desert conditions that make Pleasant Grove beautiful — low humidity, abundant sunshine, mountain air — accelerate fabric degradation in ways most homeowners don't notice until a couch looks years older than it is. UV exposure through south- and west-facing windows bleaches and brittles upholstery fibers faster at this altitude. Meanwhile, the fine alkaline dust that blows in from the valley floor works into fabric weaves and acts like microscopic sandpaper every time someone sits down. Leather is particularly vulnerable: without regular conditioning, the dry air pulls moisture out of the hide, causing cracking along seat creases and armrests within just a few years.

## Our Upholstery Cleaning Process in Pleasant Grove

Every job starts with a fiber identification step that most cleaners skip. Knowing whether you have a synthetic microfiber, a natural cotton-linen blend, a wool accent chair, or a full-grain leather sectional determines which cleaning agents and moisture levels are safe. Using the wrong method — especially over-wetting a natural fiber in a dry climate — can cause shrinkage, ring marks, or color migration that's difficult to reverse.

For fabric upholstery, we use a low-moisture hot-water extraction process: a pre-conditioner loosens embedded soil and oils, a soft-bristle grooming tool lifts the nap, and then our truck-mounted or portable extraction equipment pulls the solution and debris out before it can re-deposit. In Pleasant Grove's dry air, fabric pieces typically reach touchable dryness in two to three hours — faster than the same job in a humid climate, which is one genuine advantage of working at elevation.

For leather upholstery cleaning, we use pH-balanced leather cleaners followed by a conditioner appropriate to the hide type — full-grain, corrected-grain, and bonded leather each require a different product. We do not steam-clean leather; the heat and moisture can cause irreversible warping and dye transfer.
