## Our Mold Inspection and Testing Process in Saratoga Springs

A mold inspection isn't the same as a mold test, and conflating the two is one of the most common mistakes homeowners make. An inspection is a physical, methodical walk-through of the structure — checking moisture readings in walls, floors, and ceilings with calibrated meters, identifying visible growth, and mapping areas of concern. Testing involves collecting samples: air cassettes that capture spore counts in the breathing zone, and surface swabs or tape lifts from suspect areas that get sent to an accredited third-party laboratory.

When we arrive at a Saratoga Springs home, we start with the areas most likely to harbor hidden moisture given local conditions: crawl spaces, the band joist area where the foundation meets the framing, and any bathroom or laundry space that shares a wall with an exterior. We use thermal imaging to detect temperature differentials that indicate moisture behind surfaces — a tool that's especially useful in the stucco-exterior homes common in this area, where bulk water intrusion can travel several feet from the original entry point before causing visible damage.

Air samples are collected from the interior living space and compared against an outdoor baseline sample taken at the same time. The ratio between indoor and outdoor spore counts — and the specific species identified — tells us whether what we're seeing is a normal background level or an active amplification event inside the structure. Results from our certified lab partner typically return within 24–48 hours.
