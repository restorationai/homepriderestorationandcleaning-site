## Why Orem Homes See More Appliance Leak Damage Than You'd Expect

Utah County's hard water is the quiet culprit behind a surprising number of appliance failures in Orem. The municipal water supply here carries high mineral content, and over time that mineral buildup stresses braided steel supply lines, corrodes ice maker connections, and accelerates wear on washing machine hoses — particularly in homes built during Orem's rapid growth periods of the 1970s and 1980s. Those decades produced a lot of split-level and ranch-style construction across the city, and those floor plans often route laundry rooms directly above finished basements or crawl spaces. When a washing machine flood happens on the main floor, water finds the path of least resistance straight down into framing cavities.

Refrigerator leak cleanup is another frequent call in Orem's newer subdivisions, where side-by-side and French-door models with ice makers are common. The plastic tubing connecting to the water supply is vulnerable to kinking during installation or moving, and a pinhole drip behind a refrigerator can saturate engineered hardwood flooring — which swells and delaminates faster than solid wood — before anyone notices.

## Our Appliance Leak Cleanup Process in Orem

When we arrive, the first priority is stopping any active water source and documenting the affected area with moisture meters and thermal imaging cameras. Standing water comes out with truck-mounted extraction equipment. From there, the process depends on what's wet: laminate and engineered flooring often need removal because they trap moisture underneath and won't dry in place, while tile set on a concrete slab may dry with targeted air movers and dehumidifiers alone.

For washing machine floods that have reached a basement or crawl space, we set up desiccant or refrigerant dehumidifiers calibrated to Orem's ambient humidity levels. Because the Wasatch Front's dry climate means outdoor air is often low in moisture, we sometimes use controlled ventilation to accelerate drying — a technique that wouldn't work as reliably in a more humid climate. Moisture readings are logged daily, and drying is confirmed with documentation before any repairs begin. We work directly with homeowners and their insurance adjusters throughout, providing the moisture logs and photo documentation carriers typically require.

## Local Note: What Orem's Climate Does to Drying Timelines

One thing that surprises homeowners here: Orem's low relative humidity is an asset during drying, but it can mask how far moisture has actually traveled. When outdoor air is 15–20% relative humidity — common during Utah's dry winters — surface materials dry quickly, and a floor can feel dry to the touch while the subfloor beneath still reads 25% moisture content on a pin meter.
