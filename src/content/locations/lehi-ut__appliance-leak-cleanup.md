## Why Lehi Homes See More Appliance Leak Damage Than You'd Expect

Lehi sits in a high-desert climate where indoor humidity swings dramatically between seasons. In winter, forced-air heating drops indoor humidity low enough that supply hoses on dishwashers and washing machines dry out and crack at the fittings. In summer, condensation around refrigerator water lines and ice maker connections builds up unnoticed inside cabinetry. That thermal cycling — cold, dry winters followed by warm summers — accelerates wear on braided stainless and rubber supply lines faster than manufacturers' replacement schedules account for.

Lehi's newer construction stock adds another wrinkle. Homes built in the 2010s and early 2020s frequently use LVP (luxury vinyl plank) or engineered hardwood over OSB subfloors. Both materials trap water underneath rather than wicking it away, so a slow dishwasher leak that drips for two weeks can saturate the subfloor completely while the surface looks dry. By the time a homeowner smells something musty, moisture readings under the flooring are already in the 30–40% range — well past the threshold where mold colonization begins within 24 to 48 hours.

## Our Appliance Leak Cleanup Process in Lehi

When we arrive — typically within 60 to 90 minutes of your call — the first step is source confirmation. We don't assume the appliance you think caused the problem is the only one involved. Ice maker lines, for example, often run through multiple cabinets before reaching the refrigerator, and a pinhole leak halfway along that run can look like a dishwasher problem from the kitchen floor.

From there, the process moves through four concrete phases:

1. **Moisture mapping** — We use thermal imaging cameras and calibrated moisture meters to trace exactly where water has traveled, including under baseboards, into adjacent rooms, and beneath flooring.
2. **Controlled extraction** — Truck-mounted and portable extractors pull standing water and water trapped in flooring layers. For LVP and engineered hardwood, we use specialty drying mats that draw moisture up through the flooring without requiring demolition in every case.
3. **Structural drying** — Industrial desiccant dehumidifiers and high-velocity air movers run in a calculated pattern based on the room's square footage and the moisture readings from step one. We set and log target drywall and subfloor readings daily.
4. **Documentation and clearance** — Every moisture reading, every equipment placement, and every daily check is logged. That documentation goes directly to your insurance adjuster if you're filing a claim.

We're IICRC Certified and licensed in Utah (license #RC-25-0737), which matters when your adjuster asks for credentials before approving a scope of work.
