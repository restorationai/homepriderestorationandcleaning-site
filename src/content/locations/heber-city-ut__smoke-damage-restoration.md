## Why Heber City Homes Are Especially Vulnerable to Smoke Damage

Heber City's housing stock tells two very different stories. Newer subdivisions near the Heber Valley Airport corridor feature modern construction with tight building envelopes — which sounds protective until you realize that tight homes trap smoke-laden air and recirculate it through ductwork for days after the fire is out. Older homes closer to Main Street and the historic downtown core, many built in the mid-20th century, often have original wood-framed walls with minimal vapor barriers, meaning smoke oils soak into framing members and subflooring in ways that surface cleaning alone can't address.

Wildfire smoke adds a separate layer of complexity. The Uinta National Forest borders Wasatch County to the east and south, and fire seasons that push smoke into the valley — even without a structure fire on your property — can deposit fine particulate matter and volatile organic compounds on interior surfaces through window gaps, attic vents, and HVAC intakes. That ambient wildfire smoke cleanup is a different protocol than post-fire smoke cleanup, and conflating the two leads to incomplete results.

## Our Smoke Damage Restoration Process in Heber City

Every job starts with a room-by-room assessment using thermal imaging and air quality meters to map where smoke traveled — not just where the fire burned. Smoke follows air pressure gradients, so a fire in a garage can leave heavy residue in a second-floor bedroom closet while leaving the hallway between them relatively clean. We document everything photographically before touching a surface, which matters when your insurance adjuster needs a scope of loss.

From there, the process moves through four concrete phases:

1. **Containment and ventilation** — negative air pressure units isolate affected zones and pull contaminated air out through HEPA filtration, preventing cross-contamination to unaffected rooms.
2. **Dry residue removal** — chemical sponges and HEPA vacuuming lift loose soot before any wet cleaning begins. Applying water or cleaning agents to dry soot first smears it deeper into surfaces.
3. **Chemical neutralization** — smoke residue is acidic. We apply pH-appropriate cleaning agents calibrated to the surface type — different formulas for painted drywall, exposed wood, fabric, and HVAC components.
4. **Deodorization** — thermal fogging and hydroxyl generators address odor at the molecular level. In Heber City's colder months, when homes are sealed tight against the cold, we extend the hydroxyl treatment cycle because low humidity and cold temperatures slow the oxidation process.
