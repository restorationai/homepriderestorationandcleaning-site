## Our Carpet Cleaning Process in Pleasant Grove

Every job starts with a fiber and dye assessment before a drop of solution touches the carpet. That step matters here because Utah County homes frequently have olefin (polypropylene) carpet installed by production builders — a fiber that's sensitive to high-heat extraction and certain alkaline detergents. Once we know what we're working with, the process follows a deliberate sequence:

- **Pre-treatment** — A pH-balanced emulsifier is applied to traffic lanes and stain zones, with dwell time calibrated to fiber type and soil load.
- **Agitation** — A counter-rotating brush machine works the solution into the pile without distorting the twist in the fiber.
- **Hot-water extraction** — Truck-mounted equipment operating at 200–230°F rinses and vacuums simultaneously, pulling suspended soil, allergens, and cleaning residue in a single pass.
- **Speed drying** — High-velocity air movers reduce dry time to 2–4 hours in Pleasant Grove's low-humidity air, so you're not walking on damp carpet all day.
- **Post-cleaning inspection** — We walk the room with you under raking light to confirm stain results and fiber condition before we pack up.

For pet urine — a common call in family neighborhoods throughout the city — we use a UV light to map the contamination zone before treatment, because the visible stain rarely matches the actual affected area.
