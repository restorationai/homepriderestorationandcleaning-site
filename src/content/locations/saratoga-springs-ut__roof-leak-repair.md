## Our Roof Leak Cleanup and Repair Process

When you call, a technician leaves our Saratoga Springs location — not a regional dispatch center — and arrives on-site typically within 60 to 90 minutes. The first priority is stopping active water intrusion: we apply temporary weatherproofing to the compromised roof section so cleanup work below isn't fighting an ongoing leak.

From there, the process moves in a deliberate sequence:

- **Moisture mapping** — Thermal imaging and pin-type moisture meters trace exactly how far water has traveled through roof decking, attic insulation, ceiling assemblies, and any interior wall cavities.
- **Insulation removal** — Wet batt or blown-in insulation cannot be dried in place; it holds moisture against framing and dramatically extends drying time. We remove and bag it for disposal.
- **Structural drying** — Commercial desiccant dehumidifiers and high-velocity air movers are positioned to dry roof decking and ceiling framing from both sides where accessible. Our IICRC-certified technicians monitor readings daily and adjust equipment placement based on psychrometric data.
- **Antimicrobial treatment** — Any framing or sheathing that shows early biological growth receives an EPA-registered antimicrobial application before enclosure.
- **Repair coordination** — Once the structure is dry and documented, we coordinate or perform the finish repairs: replacing damaged drywall, repainting, reinstalling insulation to current R-value code, and completing the permanent roof repair.

All moisture readings are logged and provided to you and your insurance adjuster in a written drying report.

## Insurance Coordination for Saratoga Springs Homeowners

Most standard homeowners policies in Utah cover sudden and accidental roof leaks — a failed flashing, a wind-lifted shingle, ice dam damage — but exclude long-term seepage that should have been caught in maintenance. The distinction matters, and adjusters will look at the damage pattern to make that call. We document the loss thoroughly from the first hour on-site: timestamped photos, moisture readings, and a written scope of damage that gives your adjuster a clear picture of what happened and when.

If your neighborhood is governed by an HOA — common in several of Saratoga Springs' planned communities — we're familiar with the coordination those situations require. Some HOAs in the area require pre-approval for exterior work or have preferred vendor lists for roof repairs. We'll help you navigate that process so it doesn't slow down the interior cleanup.
