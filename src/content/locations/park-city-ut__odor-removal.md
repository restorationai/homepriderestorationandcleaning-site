## Why Park City Homes Hold Odors Differently

Most of Park City's residential stock falls into two broad categories: older historic homes near Old Town dating to the mining era, and post-1990 resort-era construction ranging from dense condo developments to large custom homes above Deer Valley. Both present odor challenges that don't show up in the Salt Lake Valley.

The historic Old Town properties often have original plaster walls, uninsulated crawl spaces, and wood-framed structures that have absorbed decades of fireplace smoke, cooking odors, and the occasional flooding from spring snowmelt. Plaster is porous in a way modern drywall isn't — it can hold odor molecules deep in the matrix, which means surface-level deodorizing sprays accomplish almost nothing. The resort-era homes, on the other hand, tend to be well-sealed for energy efficiency, which is great for heating bills but terrible when a smoke event or pet odor gets trapped. With limited air exchange, volatile organic compounds (VOCs) from smoke residue or biological sources recirculate rather than escape.

Vacation rental owners often discover a smoke or pet odor problem only when a guest complains, compressing the timeline for remediation significantly.

## Our Odor Removal and Deodorization Process in Park City

Effective odor elimination here isn't a single-step job. After an initial assessment to identify odor source categories and concentrations, the process typically moves through several stages calibrated to the specific conditions of the property.

**Source removal** comes first — no deodorization technology works reliably if the underlying source material is still present. That means removing smoke-damaged porous materials, treating pet-affected subfloor, or addressing the mold colony that's producing musty VOCs.

**Thermal fogging** is particularly effective in Park City's tightly sealed resort homes. A petroleum-based or water-based fog is heated and dispersed to penetrate the same spaces smoke or odors reached — inside wall cavities, under flooring, into HVAC ductwork. Because the fog particles are roughly the same size as smoke particles, they bond with and neutralize odor molecules in places a spray bottle will never reach.

**Hydroxyl deodorization** is our preferred method when occupants or pets need to re-enter the space quickly, or when a short-term rental property has a narrow window between guest bookings. Hydroxyl generators produce reactive hydroxyl radicals that break down odor-causing compounds without requiring the space to be vacated for extended periods, unlike ozone treatment.

**Ozone treatment** remains the most aggressive option for severe smoke odor — particularly after a structure fire or a significant wildfire smoke intrusion event. At Park City's elevation, ozone generators need to run longer to achieve effective concentrations than they would at sea level, a calibration detail that matters for getting the job done right the first time.
