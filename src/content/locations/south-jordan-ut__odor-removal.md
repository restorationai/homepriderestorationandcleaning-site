## Why South Jordan Properties Hold Odors Longer

Most of South Jordan's residential stock was built after 2000, which means open floor plans, engineered wood subfloors, and spray-foam or blown-in insulation — materials that absorb and retain odors differently than older construction. Engineered wood is especially problematic after smoke events: the adhesive layers between wood plies can off-gas for weeks after the visible soot is gone, releasing a secondary wave of smell that homeowners mistake for a new problem. Spray-foam insulation, common in the energy-efficient builds throughout the South Jordan Towne Center area, seals odor molecules inside wall cavities where ventilation alone can't reach them.

The Jordan Valley's inversion season compounds the issue. From roughly November through February, cold air traps pollutants close to the ground. A house fire or severe smoke event during an inversion period means the outdoor air you're ventilating with is already carrying particulate load — opening windows can actually slow the deodorization process rather than help it.

## Our Odor Removal and Deodorization Process in South Jordan

Every job starts with an odor mapping walkthrough. We identify source zones — not just the obvious ones like a charred kitchen or a pet-soiled carpet — but secondary absorption points: HVAC returns, attic insulation, wall cavities adjacent to the event area. In South Jordan homes with two-story great rooms, smoke stratifies and saturates the upper wall surfaces and ceiling joists in ways that single-story layouts don't produce.

From there, the method depends on what we find:

- **Thermal fogging** works well for smoke odor removal in homes where the odor has penetrated porous surfaces and framing. The fog mimics the behavior of smoke itself, following the same pathways the original odor traveled.
- **Hydroxyl deodorization** is our preferred method when occupants, pets, or sensitive electronics need to remain on-site — hydroxyls are safe to run in occupied spaces and work continuously over 24–72 hours.
- **Ozone treatment** delivers the fastest molecular-level odor elimination for vacant properties, but the home must be fully vacated during treatment and properly ventilated afterward. We time ozone cycles carefully given South Jordan's inversion conditions — running exhaust fans during a heavy inversion day can work against clearance.
- **Enzyme and encapsulant applications** address biological sources: pet urine that has wicked into concrete slab edges (common in South Jordan's slab-on-grade construction), subfloor contamination, and HVAC ductwork odor.
