South Jordan sits on the western bench of the Salt Lake Valley, where the water table runs surprisingly shallow in many subdivisions and the clay-heavy soils that dominate the 84095 ZIP code expand and contract with every freeze-thaw cycle. That soil movement, combined with the irrigation-heavy summers that keep South Jordan's lawns green, creates exactly the kind of persistent ground moisture that turns an unencapsulated crawl space into a breeding ground for mold, wood rot, and pest intrusion. Home Pride Restoration and Cleaning has been sealing crawl spaces across the valley since 1997, and the conditions here are specific enough to warrant a different approach than you'd take in a drier basin community.

## Why South Jordan Crawl Spaces Are Especially Vulnerable

The Daybreak master-planned community gets a lot of attention for its walkable design and the community lake, but what doesn't come up in the HOA newsletters is that large portions of the development were graded over former agricultural land with high clay content and residual irrigation infrastructure. Clay soil doesn't drain — it holds moisture against your foundation. Homes built on those lots in the mid-2000s often have crawl spaces where relative humidity sits above 70% for months at a stretch, even without a visible plumbing leak.

Outside of Daybreak, older neighborhoods closer to the 114th South corridor have homes built in the late 1980s and early 1990s with vented crawl spaces designed to the building codes of that era. The logic at the time was that outside air would ventilate moisture out. In Utah's climate, the opposite happens: warm summer air carries humidity into the cooler crawl space, where it condenses on joists and subfloor sheathing. By the time a homeowner notices a musty smell rising through the floorboards, the wood has often been cycling through wet-dry stress for years.

## Our Crawl Space Encapsulation Process in South Jordan

Every project starts with a moisture mapping inspection — we measure vapor emission from the soil, check wood moisture content in the floor joists, and document any existing mold or pest activity before a single roll of liner goes down. This matters in South Jordan because the clay-soil moisture problem doesn't stop at the foundation wall; it comes up through the ground. A liner that isn't properly lapped and sealed at the seams will let vapor migrate underneath it, which defeats the purpose entirely.

Once the crawl space is prepped and any remediation work is complete, we install a reinforced polyethylene vapor barrier — typically 20-mil — across the entire floor and up the foundation walls, sealed with two-sided tape and mechanical fasteners. We then assess whether a crawl space dehumidifier is warranted based on the readings we took at the start. In South Jordan's irrigation season (roughly April through October), a dehumidifier is almost always the right call. We close out every job with a post-installation humidity reading so you have a documented baseline.

Because we're IICRC Certified and licensed in Utah (License #RC-25-0737), the documentation we provide is the same format that home inspectors and real estate agents expect to see if you're selling the property.
