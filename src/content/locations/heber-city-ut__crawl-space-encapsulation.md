## Why Heber City Crawl Spaces Are Especially Vulnerable

The Heber Valley sits in a high-altitude basin ringed by mountains that funnel snowmelt toward the valley floor every spring. That geography means the soil beneath many homes in the 84032 ZIP code stays wetter, longer, than homeowners expect. Vented crawl spaces — the standard construction method in homes built here through the 1990s — were designed for climates where outside air helps dry out the space below your floor. In Heber City, outside air in March or April often carries more moisture than the crawl space itself, so ventilation works against you.

Add to that the clay-heavy soils common along the lower valley benches near Midway Road, which drain slowly and hold capillary moisture against foundation walls and footings. Homes with pier-and-beam construction — a common build style in the older sections of town closer to Main Street — often have minimal vapor barriers or none at all, leaving wood framing exposed to ground moisture year-round. The result: musty odors that migrate into living spaces, wood rot on sill plates and floor joists, and in some cases elevated humidity that feeds mold colonies before anyone notices a problem.

## Our Crawl Space Encapsulation Process in Heber City

Encapsulation is not a single product — it's a system, and every component has to work together for the seal to hold through Heber City's freeze-thaw cycles.

We start with a thorough inspection of the existing crawl space: measuring ground moisture levels, checking for standing water or drainage issues, assessing the condition of any existing vapor barrier, and documenting wood damage or mold growth that needs to be addressed before sealing. If there's active moisture intrusion from the perimeter, we address drainage first — sealing over a wet crawl space just traps the problem.

Once the space is clean and dry, we install a heavy-mil polyethylene liner (typically 20-mil reinforced) across the entire floor and up the foundation walls, sealed at seams with moisture-resistant tape and mechanically fastened at the wall perimeter. We seal all vents — a step that sometimes surprises homeowners but is consistent with modern building science and current IRC guidance for conditioned crawl spaces in cold climates like Heber City's. A properly sealed crawl space with a dehumidifier maintains more stable conditions than a vented one in this climate zone.

Finally, we install a crawl space dehumidifier sized to the square footage and drainage-plumbed so it doesn't require manual emptying. In a valley that can see 60-degree temperature swings between a January night and a July afternoon, passive vapor control alone isn't enough.
