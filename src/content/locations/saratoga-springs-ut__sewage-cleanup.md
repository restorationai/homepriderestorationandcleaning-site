## Our Sewage Cleanup and Sanitization Process in Saratoga Springs

When we arrive on-site, the first priority is containment — stopping the source if it hasn't already been addressed and establishing physical barriers to keep contaminated material from migrating into unaffected areas. From there, the process moves through five concrete phases:

1. **Extraction** — Industrial truck-mounted and portable extraction units remove standing sewage and saturated water from hard surfaces, carpet, and subfloor cavities. In Saratoga Springs homes with finished basements — which represent the majority of calls we handle here — this often means pulling carpet and pad and cutting drywall at the flood line to expose wall cavities.
2. **Removal of non-salvageable materials** — Porous materials that absorbed Category 3 water (sewage-contaminated) are bagged, documented, and removed. This is non-negotiable from both a health and an insurance documentation standpoint.
3. **Disinfection and sanitization** — EPA-registered hospital-grade disinfectants are applied to all affected surfaces, including concrete slab, framing, and HVAC returns if contamination reached them. This is not a spray-and-wipe step; dwell time matters, and we follow IICRC S500 protocol.
4. **Structural drying** — High-capacity desiccant and refrigerant dehumidifiers combined with axial air movers are positioned to dry the structural assembly, not just the surface. We monitor moisture readings daily and adjust equipment until readings return to pre-loss baselines.
5. **Post-cleanup verification** — Before we close out, we document final moisture readings and surface conditions. If your insurance adjuster or HOA requires third-party testing, we can coordinate that step.
