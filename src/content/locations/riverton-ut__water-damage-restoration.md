## Why Riverton Properties Are Vulnerable to Water Damage

The geology under Riverton works against homeowners in a few specific ways. Much of the city was developed on former agricultural land with clay-heavy soil that drains poorly. When that soil becomes saturated — whether from a prolonged rainstorm, a sprinkler line failure, or a water main break — it holds moisture against foundation walls and under slabs for days. Homes built in the rapid-growth subdivisions of the late 1990s and early 2000s along Redwood Road and the areas near South Mountain Golf Club were often constructed with standard builder-grade waterproofing that wasn't designed for repeated hydrostatic stress.

The valley's freeze-thaw cycle adds another layer of risk. Riverton sees enough sub-freezing nights each winter that supply lines running through exterior walls or unheated garages are legitimate burst-pipe candidates — and when a pipe lets go at 2 a.m., standing water can reach flooring, baseboards, and wall cavities within minutes. The longer water sits, the deeper it migrates.

## Our Water Damage Restoration Process in Riverton

Every job starts with a moisture assessment before any equipment is staged. Our IICRC-certified technicians use thermal imaging cameras and calibrated moisture meters to map exactly where water has traveled — including inside wall assemblies and under hardwood or tile that looks dry on the surface. That map drives every decision that follows.

Once we understand the full scope, water removal begins with truck-mounted extraction units capable of pulling hundreds of gallons from flooring and structural cavities. Standing water in a finished basement typically clears in under an hour; saturated subfloor assemblies take longer and require targeted extraction tools. After bulk water removal, we set industrial desiccant dehumidifiers and high-velocity air movers calibrated to the room's square footage and the materials involved. Structural drying in a typical Riverton home runs three to five days, with daily moisture readings to confirm progress and adjust equipment placement.

We document every reading and every equipment log for your insurance carrier. Home Pride holds a Utah contractor's license (#RC-25-0737) and works directly with all major insurers, so the paperwork burden on your end stays manageable.
