## Our Appliance Leak Cleanup Process in Heber City

The first thing our technicians do on arrival is stop the water source — shutting off the appliance supply valve or the main if the valve itself has failed. From there, the process follows a sequence calibrated to the specific appliance and the materials in your home:

**Assessment and moisture mapping.** We use thermal imaging cameras and calibrated moisture meters to trace exactly where water has traveled — under flooring, into wall cavities, and beneath cabinetry. Appliance leaks are deceptive because water follows the path of least resistance, often surfacing ten feet from the actual source.

**Extraction.** Truck-mounted extraction removes standing water from hard surfaces and pulls moisture from carpet and padding. For subfloor saturation — common with washing machine floods — we use specialty tools designed to draw water upward through flooring without requiring full demolition in every case.

**Drying and dehumidification.** At Heber City's elevation, ambient humidity is generally lower than along the Wasatch Front, which works in your favor during drying. We position industrial air movers and low-grain refrigerant dehumidifiers to take advantage of that dry mountain air while maintaining the controlled drying conditions the IICRC S500 standard requires.

**Documentation.** Every moisture reading is logged with timestamps and mapped to a floor plan — the format most insurance adjusters and carriers request when processing a water damage claim.
