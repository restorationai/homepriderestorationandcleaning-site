## Our Roof Leak Cleanup and Repair Process in American Fork

The first priority when we arrive is stopping the water source — not just mopping up what's already there. Once the exterior is secured, we use thermal imaging cameras to map exactly how far moisture has traveled inside the attic and ceiling assembly, because water rarely stays where you first see it. Wet insulation is removed and bagged — saturated fiberglass batts hold moisture for weeks and become a mold substrate within 24 to 48 hours in Utah's summer heat. We then set commercial-grade desiccant dehumidifiers and air movers calibrated to the specific cubic footage of your attic space, monitoring moisture readings daily until structural materials hit safe levels. Once drying is confirmed, our licensed repair crew addresses the roof itself: replacing damaged decking, re-flashing penetrations, and installing new shingles or membrane sections to match the existing roof as closely as possible. Every step is documented with photos and moisture logs, which your insurance adjuster will need.
