## Why South Jordan Homes Are Particularly Vulnerable to Appliance Leaks

South Jordan sits in the Salt Lake Valley's southwestern corridor, where the soil is dense clay-heavy hardpan just a few inches below the surface. That matters for appliance leaks because water that migrates toward exterior walls or into crawl spaces has almost nowhere to drain — it pools and saturates rather than dispersing. Homes built in the 2000s and 2010s boom years, which make up the majority of the housing stock here, were often constructed with composite subfloor panels that swell and delaminate quickly once moisture gets underneath them.

The hard, mineral-rich water delivered through Jordan Valley Water Conservancy District lines also accelerates wear on appliance hoses and fittings. Calcium and magnesium deposits build up inside braided steel washing machine supply lines and refrigerator ice maker connections, making them brittle over time. A line that looks fine from the outside can crack at a fitting with almost no warning. Water heater supply connections are especially prone to this — and a slow drip at the base of a water heater in a finished utility closet can saturate drywall and framing for weeks before anyone notices the smell.

## Our Appliance Leak Cleanup Process in South Jordan

The first thing our technicians do on arrival is identify and confirm the source — not just mop up what's visible. A dishwasher leak that appears contained to the kitchen often tracks along the toe-kick cavity and under adjacent cabinetry before it surfaces. We use thermal imaging cameras and calibrated moisture meters to map the full extent of saturation before any equipment is placed.

Once the moisture boundary is established, we extract standing water with truck-mounted and portable extraction units, then set a drying system calibrated to the affected materials. Engineered hardwood and LVP common in South Jordan's newer builds require lower-temperature, higher-airflow drying — aggressive heat can cause irreversible cupping or delamination. We use desiccant or refrigerant dehumidifiers depending on the season and structure, and we monitor moisture readings daily until affected materials reach dry standard. Documentation — photos, moisture logs, equipment placement records — is compiled throughout for your insurance claim.
