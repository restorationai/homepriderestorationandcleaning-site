## Why Park City Properties Generate More Debris Than You'd Expect

At 7,000 feet, Park City's climate is hard on everything. Freeze-thaw cycles crack plastic storage bins, warp wooden furniture left in unheated garages, and corrode metal shelving in crawl spaces. Properties that sit vacant between ski season and summer often accumulate moisture damage that renders stored items unsalvageable — mattresses, cardboard boxes, upholstered furniture. Add in the region's active renovation culture (older homes near Historic Main Street and the lower Prospector area get updated constantly to meet short-term rental standards), and you end up with demo debris, old appliances, and construction cast-offs that can't just be rolled to the curb.

Summit County's waste hauling rules also matter here. Certain materials — old paint cans, propane canisters from outdoor grills, treated lumber — can't go in a standard dumpster, and the county transfer station has specific drop-off requirements. Knowing what's regulated before a truck is loaded saves a trip back.
