## Why Park City Carpets Accumulate Damage Faster

The combination of heavy recreational traffic and high-altitude UV exposure creates a one-two punch that breaks down carpet fibers faster than most homeowners expect. Ski boots, snowboard bags, and wet gear grind fine silica-laden grit deep into pile — the kind of particulate that doesn't vacuum out and acts like sandpaper on fiber roots with every footstep. That same dry mountain air causes carpet fibers to become brittle and static-prone, which actually attracts and holds fine dust rather than releasing it.

Vacation rental properties and short-term lodges see this compounded by high turnover: a unit that cycles through dozens of guests between October and April can accumulate six months of residential wear in a single ski season. Stains from après-ski snacks, boot wax, and sports drinks set faster in the low-humidity environment because liquid evaporates before it can be blotted — leaving concentrated residue bonded to fiber rather than sitting on the surface.

## Our Deep Carpet Cleaning Process, Calibrated for the Mountain Environment

We start every job with a dry soil extraction pass — a step we weight more heavily in Park City than at lower elevations because of that silica-grit load. Pulling abrasive particles out before introducing any moisture protects fiber integrity and makes the hot-water extraction phase dramatically more effective.

Hot-water extraction (often called steam cleaning) follows, using truck-mounted equipment powerful enough to reach full operating pressure and temperature at altitude — a detail that matters because some portable units lose efficiency above 5,000 feet. We pre-treat stains individually rather than applying a blanket pre-spray, which lets us match the chemistry to the stain type: ski wax responds to a solvent-based treatment; red wine or beet-based après-ski stains need an oxidizing agent; pet accidents require an enzyme formula that breaks down uric acid crystals.

Drying time in Park City's low-humidity air is actually faster than in the Wasatch Front valleys, which is a genuine advantage. Most residential rooms are walk-ready within two to four hours. We leave air movers on-site for commercial jobs or heavily saturated areas to keep that timeline consistent even in larger spaces.
