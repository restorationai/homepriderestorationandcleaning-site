## Why Lehi Properties See Tile & Grout Problems

Lehi sits at the edge of the Utah Lake basin, and the soil here is notably alkaline and fine-grained. When wind picks up across the undeveloped parcels still scattered through the 84043 corridor, that dust finds its way inside through door sweeps and HVAC returns. Over months, grout lines in entryways, mudrooms, and kitchen floors shift from their original color to a dull gray-tan that looks like staining but is actually embedded mineral particulate.

The other factor is water hardness. Utah County water consistently tests among the hardest in the state — calcium and magnesium deposits build up in grout pores and along tile edges, leaving a chalky haze that dulls even high-gloss porcelain. Homeowners often assume the tile itself is worn; in most cases, a professional hot-water extraction and acid-rinse cycle restores the original finish without any tile replacement.

Lehi's housing stock also skews newer, which sounds like an advantage, but many developments built between 2005 and 2018 used large-format porcelain tile with unsanded grout joints under 1/8 inch — joints that seal poorly and show traffic patterns within a year or two of installation.

## Our Tile & Grout Cleaning Process in Lehi

Every job starts with a pH test of the grout and a hardness assessment of the water supply at the property. That tells us whether to lead with an alkaline pre-treatment (for organic soils — grease, food residue, pet dander) or an acidic descaler (for mineral buildup). We don't pick one and hope.

After pre-treatment dwell time, we use truck-mounted rotary extraction equipment running water at 200–230°F. The spinning jets reach into grout joints at angles a mop or scrub brush can't, and the simultaneous vacuum extraction pulls the emulsified soil out rather than spreading it. On natural stone — travertine is common in Lehi bathrooms — we drop the temperature and pressure to protect the surface.

Once the floor is clean and dry, we apply a penetrating silane-siloxane sealer to grout lines. This is the step most DIY and discount services skip, and it's the difference between results that last two years and results that last six months. We finish with a color-seal option for grout that has been permanently stained or is simply a color the homeowner wants to change — a popular choice in older Thanksgiving Point-area homes where builder-grade beige grout has aged poorly.
