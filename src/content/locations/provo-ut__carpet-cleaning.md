## Local Note: Provo's Dry Air Speeds Drying — But Hides Residue Risk

One thing we've learned working in Utah Valley for nearly three decades: Provo's low relative humidity means carpets feel dry to the touch faster than in coastal or Midwest markets — sometimes within three to four hours of extraction. That's genuinely good news for turnaround time. The risk is that some technicians (and most rental machines) interpret "feels dry" as "job done" and skip a final grooming pass. In dry climates, cleaning solution residue that isn't fully extracted can wick back to the surface as the carpet dries, leaving a stiff, slightly sticky texture that actually attracts more soil faster than before the cleaning. We do a final low-moisture rinse pass on every job specifically to prevent that resoiling effect — it's a step that matters more in Provo's climate than it would in a humid market.

If your carpet has been cleaned before and seemed to get dirty again unusually fast, residue resoiling is likely the reason.
