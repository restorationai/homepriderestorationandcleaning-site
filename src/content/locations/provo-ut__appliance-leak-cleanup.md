## Our Appliance Leak Cleanup Process in Provo

When a technician arrives, the first priority is stopping any ongoing water source — shutting off the supply valve behind the appliance or at the main if the valve is corroded and won't seat. From there, the process moves in a specific order:

1. **Moisture mapping** — thermal imaging and pin-type meters document exactly how far water has traveled under flooring, into adjacent wall cavities, and beneath cabinetry. This step matters especially in Provo's older ranch-style homes where a single kitchen appliance leak can track under a continuous subfloor into an adjacent dining room.
2. **Extraction** — truck-mounted and portable extractors pull standing and absorbed water from hard flooring, carpet, and pad. For tile-over-slab floors common in many Provo kitchens, drying mats applied directly to the tile surface draw moisture up through the grout lines without requiring tile removal in most cases.
3. **Structural drying** — LGR dehumidifiers and high-velocity air movers are positioned based on the moisture map, not guesswork. Drying logs are recorded daily so your insurance adjuster has documentation.
4. **Antimicrobial treatment** — applied to any surface that was in contact with water for more than a few hours, particularly cabinet interiors and subfloor sheathing.
5. **Final clearance readings** — equipment stays until materials return to acceptable moisture levels, typically three to five days depending on construction type.
