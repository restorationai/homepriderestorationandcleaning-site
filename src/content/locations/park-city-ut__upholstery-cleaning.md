The altitude factor matters practically, not just theoretically. Calibrating dwell time, moisture volume, and airflow is something our IICRC-certified technicians adjust specifically for mountain-elevation jobs.

## Our Upholstery Cleaning Process in Park City

Every job starts with a fiber identification step — we test the fabric type before any chemistry touches the piece. Wool and natural blends common in older Park City homes require pH-neutral solutions; aggressive alkaline cleaners can cause irreversible shrinkage or color shift in those fibers. Synthetic performance fabrics used in many Canyons-area vacation properties can tolerate a wider range of extraction methods but often need a pre-treatment enzyme soak to break down the biological load from heavy rental use.

For leather upholstery cleaning, we use a multi-stage process: a gentle pH-balanced cleaner to lift surface soil, a conditioning treatment to restore the oils that Park City's dry air pulls out of the hide, and a protective finish appropriate to the leather type. Aniline leathers — common in higher-end mountain homes — are particularly vulnerable to the UV intensity at this elevation and benefit from a UV-inhibiting conditioner applied after cleaning.

Fabric pieces go through hot-water extraction calibrated to the fiber type, followed by controlled drying with directional airflow. We do not leave jobs until we've verified that cushion cores, not just surface fabric, are reading dry on our moisture meters.
