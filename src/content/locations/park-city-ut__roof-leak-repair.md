## Why Park City Properties Are Especially Vulnerable to Roof Leaks

The elevation alone changes the math. Homes in the 84060 ZIP code sit at roughly 7,000 feet, where freeze-thaw cycles are more frequent and more extreme than anywhere along the Wasatch Front. A single week in late winter can swing from 40°F afternoons to single-digit nights, and that repeated expansion and contraction works flashing loose, opens seams in low-slope roof sections, and drives ice dams deep under shingles. Many of the older chalets and ski-in properties built in the 1970s and early 1980s — a housing stock that's common in the historic mining district and in neighborhoods near the Park City Mountain base — were designed with dramatic roof pitches that look great but create valleys where debris and ice accumulate. When those valleys fail, water doesn't just drip; it channels.

Condominiums and townhome developments add another layer of complexity. Shared rooflines mean one unit's leak can migrate laterally through insulation and framing into a neighboring unit before either owner notices moisture. We've extracted water from attic cavities and ceiling assemblies in multi-unit buildings where the actual breach point was two units away from the visible damage.

## Our Roof Leak Cleanup and Repair Process in Park City

When you call (801) 995-2437, the first question we ask is whether the leak is still active. If it is, our priority on arrival is stopping ongoing intrusion — tarping the breach or applying temporary flashing — before we touch anything inside. Letting interior cleanup begin while water is still entering just extends the drying timeline and the bill.

Once the source is controlled, we follow a structured interior restoration sequence:

- **Moisture mapping** — Thermal imaging and calibrated moisture meters trace exactly how far water has traveled through ceiling drywall, insulation batts, and any structural sheathing. In attic spaces, we check rafter bays and ridge boards, not just the visible decking.
- **Controlled demolition** — Saturated drywall and insulation can't be dried in place; trying to do so traps moisture and feeds mold. We remove only what's necessary, document everything for your insurance adjuster, and protect surrounding finishes.
- **Commercial drying** — IICRC-certified drying protocols guide our equipment placement. At Park City's altitude, evaporation rates differ from sea-level calculations, so we adjust dehumidifier settings and air mover positioning accordingly — a detail that matters when you're trying to hit dryness targets in a reasonable timeframe.
- **Reconstruction** — Once moisture readings confirm the structure is dry, we rebuild: insulation, drywall, texture, and paint matched to the existing finish.
