## Why Park City Properties See Unusual Storm Damage Patterns

The same geography that makes Park City one of the best ski destinations in the country also makes it a target for weather events that don't follow Wasatch Front norms. Storms tracking in from the northwest can stall against the Uinta foothills and dump precipitation for hours longer than the forecast suggests. Old Town's historic district — many homes built in the late 1800s and early 1900s during the silver mining era — carries original wood framing, true-dimension lumber, and unreinforced masonry chimneys that respond to wind and impact loading very differently than modern stick-frame construction. A fallen cottonwood branch that would dent a modern composite roof can split a century-old shake substrate clean through.

At the same time, newer resort-adjacent developments like those near the Canyons Village area often feature large glass panels, steep-pitch rooflines, and architectural overhangs designed for aesthetics rather than load-shedding. Those overhangs catch wind like sails. Ice dams form at the eave transitions, and when they release, the meltwater follows the path of least resistance — straight into wall cavities and subfloor systems.

## Our Storm Damage Restoration Process in Park City

The first thing we do on any storm call is a rapid exterior and interior triage — roof, windows, doors, and any penetrations where water is actively entering. We document everything photographically before touching a single piece of debris, because your insurance adjuster will need that record. Once the structure is stabilized with emergency tarping or board-up as needed, we move to moisture mapping with thermal imaging and calibrated moisture meters. In Park City's climate, ambient humidity swings dramatically between a sunny afternoon and a cold night, so we take multiple readings over time rather than relying on a single snapshot.

Drying at elevation requires adjusted equipment settings. The lower atmospheric pressure at 7,000 feet means standard desiccant and refrigerant dehumidifiers perform differently than they do in Salt Lake City — we account for that in our equipment deployment. Structural drying typically runs 3 to 5 days depending on material type, with daily monitoring logs we share with you and your adjuster. Once the structure is dry and cleared, reconstruction scoping begins, and we coordinate directly with your insurance carrier to keep the timeline moving.
