## Restoration emergencies common in American Fork

American Fork sits at roughly 4,900 feet on the Wasatch Front bench, and that elevation creates a specific set of conditions that drive restoration calls. Winters bring hard freeze-thaw cycles — temperatures that drop into the single digits overnight and climb above freezing by afternoon. That daily cycling is exactly what splits copper supply lines in uninsulated garage walls and crawl spaces, especially in the ranch-style homes built throughout the city during the 1970s and 1980s. Spring snowmelt off Mount Timpanogos pushes the water table up, and older homes in lower-lying sections near American Fork Creek can see groundwater intrusion through foundation walls that looked fine all winter.

The city's housing stock is also a factor. Neighborhoods platted before 1985 often have original polybutylene or galvanized supply lines, and many of those homes have finished basements — which means water has more square footage to damage before anyone notices it. Newer construction in master-planned developments tends to have tighter building envelopes, which is good for energy efficiency but can trap moisture and accelerate mold colonization after even a minor leak. Mold can begin to establish within 24 to 48 hours of a water event in a closed, humid space.

## Services we provide in American Fork

### Water damage restoration

Water damage in American Fork most often traces back to three sources: frozen or aging supply lines, appliance failures (water heaters and washing machine hoses are the most common culprits), and storm-driven moisture intrusion through window wells and foundation cracks. We extract standing water, use thermal imaging to find moisture hidden inside walls and under flooring, and set industrial-grade drying equipment calibrated to Utah County's low ambient humidity — which actually accelerates drying compared to more humid climates, but only if the equipment is sized correctly for the affected area.

### Fire and smoke damage restoration

Smoke residue is acidic and keeps damaging surfaces long after the flames are out. Cooking fires, chimney fires, and garage fires are the most frequent calls we receive in American Fork. We assess the difference between dry smoke residue and wet smoke residue — they require different cleaning chemistry — and we address odor at the molecular level rather than masking it. Structural drying often runs parallel to smoke remediation when firefighting water is involved.

### Mold remediation

Because so many American Fork homes have finished basements and crawl spaces, mold is often well-established before it's visible. We follow IICRC S520 protocols, contain the affected area, use HEPA filtration, and remove compromised materials. We do not make medical claims about air quality, but we do leave the space dry, clean, and documentable for your insurance carrier or a future buyer's inspector.

### Biohazard and specialty cleaning

Unattended deaths, sewage backups, and hoarding cleanups require both technical training and a calm, professional approach. Our technicians are trained to OSHA bloodborne pathogen standards and carry the appropriate PPE and disposal documentation.
