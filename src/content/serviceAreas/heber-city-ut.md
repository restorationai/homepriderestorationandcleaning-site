## Restoration emergencies common in Heber City

Heber City sits at roughly 5,600 feet in a high-mountain valley that funnels cold air off the Wasatch and Uinta ranges. That elevation and geography create a specific set of conditions that drive restoration calls year after year.

Winters here are long and hard. Freeze-thaw cycles between November and March regularly split supply lines in crawl spaces and attics of older ranch-style homes, and ice dams on steeper-pitched roofs push meltwater under shingles and into wall cavities before a homeowner ever notices a stain on the ceiling. Spring snowmelt — especially in wet years — raises the water table quickly enough that basement slabs in lower-lying neighborhoods can wick moisture for weeks after the ground thaws.

The valley's housing stock adds another layer of complexity. A significant share of Heber City's residential properties are vacation rentals or second homes, which means a slow leak behind a vanity can go undetected for an entire season. By the time an owner or property manager walks in, mold can colonize drywall in as little as 24–48 hours under the right humidity conditions — and Heber's summer monsoon moisture makes those conditions common from July through September.

## Services we provide in Heber City

### Water damage restoration

### Fire and smoke damage cleanup

Wood-burning stoves and fireplaces are common in Heber Valley homes, and chimney fires or flue malfunctions are a recurring cause of smoke damage calls we receive from the area. Smoke residue from a contained chimney event can penetrate HVAC ductwork and settle into insulation within hours. Our crews use thermal fogging, hydroxyl generators, and HEPA-filtered air scrubbers to address odor at the source rather than masking it.

### Mold remediation
